Discovery Call Guide

Anthony's conversation framework for insurance brokerages — from coffee chat to qualified opportunity. Use before, during, and immediately after every call.

Pre-Meeting Research Checklist

Rule: Never go into a discovery call without completing at least 8 of these. The more you know before you walk in, the less selling you have to do inside the room.
  • Find the firm on LinkedIn — check headcount, growth trajectory, recent posts LinkedIn
  • Check their website — what classes of business do they specialise in? Any claims or awards mentioned? Research
  • Google the MD/director you're meeting — any press, interviews, industry association roles? Research
  • Check FCA Register for their permissions — any restrictions, recent changes? Prep
  • Note their approximate GWP tier (small <£5M / mid £5–25M / large £25M+) from headcount and client types Research
  • Check if they use a network (Brokerbility, Broker Insights, Acturis) — look for public mentions Research
  • Note 1–2 genuine observations to open with — recent news, a LinkedIn post, an award, a market challenge specific to their niche Opener
  • Know the relevant market stat for their niche: 72% of UK brokers still on manual workflows, avg 23% renewal leakage Data
  • Prepare a relevant case study from memory — similar niche, quantified result, 3 sentences max Proof
  • Check if any mutual connections (LinkedIn) — can be a natural opener LinkedIn
  • Review recent Lloyd's / IUA market bulletins or FCA news relevant to their class of business Market
  • Write down 3 diagnosis questions you specifically want answered in this meeting Questions

What to Bring / Have Ready

📊
The Diagnostic Tool
Have axontra.co.uk/diagnostic ready on your phone. Offer to run it together. It quantifies the problem in 3 minutes — before you've pitched anything.
📋
One Relevant Case Study
Memorise one result: firm type, the specific problem, the outcome in numbers. Don't use a printout — tell it. "We had a mid-market commercial broker in Manchester…"
🧮
Back-of-envelope ROI calc
GWP × 2.3% renewal leakage = £X they're losing now. Have the formula ready. If they tell you their GWP, you can quote their number immediately.
📱
LinkedIn — Connected
Connect with them on LinkedIn before the meeting if you haven't. Mention something from their recent post — genuine engagement, not sycophantic.
🤐
Nothing to Sell
The worst thing you can walk in with is a product to push. Walk in with questions. The only agenda is understanding their operation — the pitch comes later, if it fits.
📝
3 Specific Questions Written
Your three most important diagnosis questions, written before you walk in. Not memorised generics — specific to what you know about this firm.

Coffee Conversation Framework

This is not a sales call. It's a conversation between two people who understand the insurance market. Your job is to ask questions, listen, and find out if there's a problem worth solving. If there is — and only if there is — you mention Axontra.
1
Opening — Establish Peer Credibility
First 5 minutes
"I appreciate the time. I've been working with commercial brokers for a while now — quite a few in [their niche / region]. I wanted to have a proper conversation rather than pitch anything. Tell me about [Business] — how long have you been in commercial?"
💡 You're positioning yourself as a peer who knows the market, not a vendor. The question turns the floor over to them immediately. Let them talk.
2
Diagnosis — Understand Their Operation
Minutes 5–15
"What does your renewal process look like at the moment — how far out do you start chasing, and how do you track where things are?"
"Where do you find the biggest friction in the submission process — is it getting the right info from clients, or is it more on the underwriter side?"
"If you could change one thing about how the operation runs day-to-day, what would it be?"
💡 These three questions cover: renewal (where leakage happens), submissions (where inefficiency lives), and operations (what they actually care about). Don't ask all three at once — let conversations flow naturally to each one.
3
Quantify — Make the Problem Concrete
Minutes 15–22
"What's your approximate renewal retention rate? Most commercial brokers I talk to are somewhere around 77–82%. Does that sound about right for you?"
"If you brought that up by 5 points — at your GWP — what does that look like in income terms?"
💡 You're not making up numbers — you're helping them quantify what they already know is a problem. The calculation is theirs. You're just asking the question. The formula: GWP × lost retention % × avg commission rate.
4
Bridge — Connect Problem to Solution (Only If Earned)
Minutes 22–28
"What you're describing is pretty common — the gap between what the system can track and what actually needs attention in the next two weeks. That's exactly the problem we built Axontra to solve."
"We work specifically with commercial brokers — not software generalists. The system surfaces the renewals at risk, prioritises submission quality, and flags the accounts where your team should be spending time."
💡 Only bridge if they've confirmed the problem exists. If they said "actually our retention is great and the operation runs fine" — don't force it. This coffee becomes a relationship conversation instead. That's fine.
5
Proof — One Specific Story
Minutes 28–33
"We've got a mid-market broker in the Lloyd's market — about 18 people, mixed commercial book. When they came to us they were losing roughly £180k/year in renewal leakage they didn't even know about. Twelve weeks after implementation: 14 point retention improvement, 40% faster submission turnaround. Their largest property account renewed that they'd written off."
💡 One story, three sentences, specific numbers. Don't list features — tell a result. The most credible thing you can say is "here's exactly what happened."
6
Close — Always End with a Concrete Next Step
Final 5 minutes
"Would it be worth doing a proper diagnostic together — it's a 20-minute session, I'd run the numbers on your actual book and you'd see exactly where the gaps are. No obligation. If nothing comes of it, you at least have the benchmarking data."
💡 The diagnostic is the close. It's valuable in itself — they get real numbers. It also means the next conversation starts with evidence, not a pitch. Never leave without a date or a "yes, send me the link."

Insurance-Specific Objections

"We already use Acturis / Applied / SSP." Technology▼
"Acturis is a great system for transaction management — we integrate with it. What Axontra does is the layer above that: surfacing the accounts that need attention across your book, before a renewal is lost or a submission goes cold. Most brokers on Acturis tell me it's brilliant at recording what happened, but it doesn't proactively tell you what's at risk right now. Is that a fair description of how you use it?"
💡 Never criticise their current system — reframe Axontra as additive, not replacement. The integration angle removes the "more disruption" objection immediately.
"We're not really at the size where this makes sense." Fit▼
"Honestly, the brokers where we see the highest ROI are in the 10–30 account manager range — where there's enough complexity to lose track, but not enough headcount to have a dedicated analytics team. You're right in that sweet spot. The renewal leakage problem doesn't scale with size — it scales with complexity. What's your split between commercial and personal lines?"
💡 Reframe 'too small' into 'ideal size.' The question at the end shifts them back to diagnosis mode.
"We had a bad experience with a data project / IT rollout." Risk▼
"That's probably the most common thing I hear — and usually it was a generic software vendor who didn't understand the brokerage operation. Axontra is insurance-specific, and the implementation is 6 weeks with dedicated support throughout. We don't go live until you're satisfied. The diagnostic I mentioned actually starts before we touch any of your systems — it's just a conversation about your data, so you see the value before any commitment."
💡 Acknowledge the pain, differentiate on specificity, reduce perceived risk with the diagnostic-first approach.
"The FCA/GDPR situation means we can't share client data easily." Compliance▼
"That's a legitimate concern and we take it seriously. Axontra was built for FCA-regulated firms from day one — we're fully GDPR-compliant, we operate on a data processing agreement, and all data stays within UK jurisdiction. We can walk through the data governance model in detail before anything moves. Most of our clients found that was actually simpler than they expected."
💡 Don't dismiss the concern — validate it and offer the data governance detail proactively. Have the DPA framework ready for the next conversation.
"What's this going to cost — we don't have budget for another SaaS subscription." Budget▼
"I'd rather not quote a number until we've actually done the diagnostic — because the number only makes sense in context of what you're currently losing. If your retention is 82% and we bring it to 86% on a £10M GWP book at 10% commission, that's £40k additional income. The platform cost is a fraction of that. But let's get the numbers right first — then the question becomes whether the ROI makes sense, not whether the cost is high."
💡 Delay the price conversation until ROI is established. Never give a price before value is clear.
"We're going through a merger / acquisition at the moment." Timing▼
"Actually that's often the best time to have this conversation — because you're about to integrate two books of business and two sets of data. The brokers that come out of acquisitions fastest are the ones who have the operational intelligence in place first. Would it be useful to think about the diagnostic as a way to baseline both books before the integration?"
💡 Turn a timing objection into a reason to move now. Acquisitions create exactly the kind of operational complexity Axontra addresses.

Post-Call Actions

Within 1hr Send a LinkedIn connection request with a note referencing one specific thing from the conversation — not generic
Within 2hrs Email follow-up: 3 sentences — what you discussed, what you said you'd send, one concrete next step with a date
Same day Log the conversation in CRM: firm name, GWP tier, key problem identified, next step, follow-up date
Within 24hrs Send the diagnostic link with a personalised one-liner about why it's relevant to their specific situation
Day 3 If no response to diagnostic: WhatsApp (if they use it) or email — 1 sentence, soft nudge, reference what they said
Day 7 Send a relevant industry article or market insight — no pitch, pure value. Shows you're thinking about their market
Day 14 If still no response: final short email — "completely understand if the timing isn't right, happy to reconnect in a few months"
The follow-up email template: "Good to talk earlier. As promised, I'm sending the [item you mentioned]. Whenever you're ready to run the diagnostic on your book — just reply and we'll set up 20 minutes. Otherwise, happy to reconnect when timing is better."
